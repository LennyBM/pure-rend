### 3. The Local SEO Strategic Framework

To capture the 46% of all Google searches that carry local intent, tradespeople must implement a hyper-local SEO strategy [32]. 

**Google Business Profile (GBP) for Service Area Businesses (SABs)**
*   **Address Configuration:** Pure service-area businesses (like plasterers who travel to clients) must hide their physical address on Google to avoid suspensions [33-35]. 
*   **Service Areas:** Define up to 20 specific service areas (cities or postcodes) within a maximum 2-hour driving radius [36-38].
*   **Review Velocity:** Proximity is less critical for SABs; Google ranks based on prominence and relevance. Generate a steady stream of reviews and explicitly ask clients to mention the specific city and service (e.g., "Great lime rendering job in Falmouth") [39-42].

**Local Landing Page Architecture**
Do not use duplicate "doorway pages" that simply swap out town names [43, 44]. Pages must be genuinely localized and follow the "Four Ps" (Product, Price, Promotion, Place) [45, 46].
*   **URL Structure:** Maintain a clean two-level hierarchy, preferably `domain.com/[service]/[city]` (e.g., `domain.com/plastering/truro`) [47].
*   **Content:** Incorporate localized social proof (reviews from that town), mentions of specific local landmarks or neighborhoods, and photos of jobs completed in the area [48-50].
*   **Concentric Growth:** Start by dominating your home city and 2-3 adjacent "Tier 1" locations before scaling outward [51, 52].

**Mobile Optimization & Conversion Rate Optimization (CRO)**
*   Over 60% of local searches occur on mobile devices. Websites must load in under 2.5 seconds (Largest Contentful Paint) [53]. 
*   Implement frictionless communication: prominent click-to-call buttons, short contact forms (3-5 fields max), and sticky headers so contact info remains visible while scrolling [54-56].

### 4. Technical Infrastructure: Schema Markup

Schema markup (structured data) translates your website's content into a machine-readable format for search engines, increasing eligibility for "Rich Results" (star ratings, FAQs, pricing) and AI-driven search platforms [57-60]. 

**Essential Schema Types:**
*   **LocalBusiness / HomeAndConstructionBusiness / Plumber:** The core schema for local SEO. Must include the exact business name, phone number, opening hours, accepted payment methods, and geographic coordinates (latitude/longitude) [57, 61-66].
*   **Service Schema:** Details specific offerings (e.g., "External Wall Insulation," "Venetian Plaster") alongside the service area to capture high-intent, long-tail queries [60, 61, 67, 68].
*   **Review & AggregateRating:** Displays star ratings in search results, heavily boosting click-through rates [61, 69, 70].
*   **FAQPage Schema:** Add to local landing pages to address common questions (e.g., "Do I need planning permission for K-Rend in a conservation area?"). This captures Voice Search traffic and "People Also Ask" snippets [61, 71-73].

**Implementation:**
*   Use the **JSON-LD** format placed in the `<head>` section of the HTML [74-77].
*   Ensure **NAP (Name, Address, Phone)** consistency across the schema, website, GBP, and all external citations. Inconsistencies erode search engine trust [26, 78, 79].
*   Validate code using Google's Rich Results Test and Schema.org Validator prior to publishing [74, 80].
